Reduced Intake of Animals During COVID-19 Helped Shelters Data Shows

An image of a animal shelter. Photo Credit: Evan Frost | MPR News

Byline: Paige Perez

October 7, 2021

"If shelters are looking to have more success, last year makes the case for reduced intake." - Marie Abbondanza, Shelter Animals Count

During the onset of the pandemic’s recovery, headlines about pet owners returning their beloved animals back to shelters caused a frenzy. But recent data doesn’t support that trend. In fact, shelters are following normal pre-pandemic trends this year and that is partially accredited to lower rates of intake. Shelters that use their resources to care for fewer animals are seeing higher success and steady adoption rates.

Shelter Animals Count, a nonprofit organization that produces The National Database of shelter animal statistics, released their COVID-19 Impact Report last month. The report compares data from the first six months (Jan. to June) of the last three years: 2019, 2020, and 2021. The Report represents data collected from 341 shelter and rescue organizations as of July 12, 2021.

In 2019, the adoption rate was 53%. By 2020, it increased to 58%. In 2021, it remained 58%.

In 2019, the shelter euthanasia rate was 11%. By 2020, it decreased to 8%. In 2021, it decreased to 7%.

In 2019, the relinquish by owner rate was 53%. By 2020, it decreased to 47%. In 2021, it remained 47%.

In 2019, the adoption rate was 18%. By 2020, it increased to 19%. In 2021, it remained 19%.

Summary: Intake

Categories of intake: stray, relinquish by owner, transfer in, other intake, and owner-intended euthanasia.

The report indicates that 2021 did not see a major influx of animals entering shelters. Instead, only a 0.56% increase in intake from 2020 was reported.

Summary: Live Outcomes

Categories of live outcomes: adoption, transfer out, return to owner, return to field, and other live outcomes.

While keeping in mind that fewer pets are coming into shelters, the pets that do come are seeing more of them be adopted.

Summary: Other Outcomes

Other outcome: shelter euthanasia, owner-intended euthanasia, died in care and lost.

Shelter euthanasia dropped -16.9% from the height of the pandemic and -53.9% since pre-pandemic.
